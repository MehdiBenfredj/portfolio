Every couple of years I burn the toolchain to the ground. Here's what survived this round and what didn't.

## What survived

- **Plain text.** Markdown files. Folders. Grep. The stuff that worked in 1995 still works.
- **One editor.** I keep telling myself I'll switch. I never do.
- **Git.** Obviously.

## What didn't

- The dotfiles repo I'd been dragging since college. It had layers of mud nobody could explain.
- A complicated terminal multiplexer setup that I used twice a year.
- All my custom keybindings. Started over with defaults. Have not missed any of them.

## The lesson

The thing you think makes you fast is usually the thing slowing you down. Defaults are underrated.
