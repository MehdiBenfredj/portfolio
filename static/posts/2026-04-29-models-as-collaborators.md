Spent the morning pair-programming with an LLM and realized the difference between a tool and a collaborator is whether it pushes back.

The good ones say "are you sure?" before doing the dumb thing. The bad ones just do the dumb thing faster.

I keep thinking about this in the context of every other tool I use. The text editor doesn't push back. The compiler does — it refuses bad code. The compiler is more of a collaborator than the editor, in some weird way.
